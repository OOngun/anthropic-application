#### Grant Categories (Ongun's Innovation)

**3. Priority Grants (Community Infrastructure)**
- Purpose: Fill critical ecosystem gaps
- Example: Funded a native data analytics platform because the only alternative (Dune) cost over $500,000. The granted project was adopted by the internal BI/data team AND by ecosystem builders as a community tool.

### PROJECT 3: Financial Product Design (Current)

#### What It Is
- A lending and borrowing protocol with a counterparty/insurance model
- NOT a traditional DeFi protocol — has a regulated, structured approach

#### The Core Thesis
- **Supply side (Bitcoin holders):** People who are long Bitcoin don't want to sell, but they need short-term liquidity and guarantees that their principal won't be lost. That's the KEY requirement.
  - Primary target: Bitcoin miners. Their business model literally involves receiving Bitcoin in the future. They need to expand rigs and operations NOW, and want to use their existing Bitcoin as collateral.
- **Demand side (Stablecoin holders):** People who just want yield on their stablecoins.
- **The Hook:** A counterparty model where RootstockLabs works with both parties and takes on the counterparty risk, using blockchain as infrastructure.

#### Why This Is Different
- Existing protocols (Aave, Morpho) have no accountability when there is a hack
- Institutions don't operate like this — they need guarantees
- RootstockLabs is proposing to set up regulated entities and structure like a proper fund, taking on counterparty risk to provide guarantees
- The insurance/counterparty model is the differentiator that attracts institutional liquidity

#### Strategic Implications
- Because the model has a counterparty, users don't care what specific tokens are used on the back end
- This solves the "cold start problem" — they don't need to convince anyone to adopt specific tokens
- Liquidity is attracted through the insurance model, not through token incentives
- The company uses its own blockchain infrastructure on the back end, but that's an implementation detail, not a selling point

#### Ongun's Role
- He is "more the operations guy who is pulling together" the initiative
- Working with the executive team on scoping
- Speaking to customers and doing discovery
- Generating leads through the portfolio
- He describes this as the "head of financial design" work, alongside the executive team
