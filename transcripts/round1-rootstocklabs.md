### On the Strategic Pivot & Product Design

**Q: Tell me about the product design work and the strategic pivot**

"The product design is something that I am leading. In fact, I pitched alongside my CFO. So I pitched and created the presentation materials and ideation with my CFO. He then pitched our board of directors that we should be pivoting as a company away from a pure technology player to a financial player. This is the key thesis.

I did an analysis and report that essentially our growth strategy was based around doing campaigns and paying for integrations, which I was also a part of through the grant program. I did many of the strategic integrations with the top tier 1 DEXs, bridges, to increase interoperability.

What I did was write the initial report of interoperability issues of our blockchain. Previously, it was thought that if we increase interoperability, it makes it easy for users to exit the ecosystem and that we wouldn't have a moat. When I did a report, I basically said that the entire market is moving towards interoperability and away from the bubbles that exist. I created a targeted list alongside our head of DeFi and business development to target the likes of Layer Zero, Uniswap, and all of these were achieved and I was part of not only the capital allocation process, but the negotiation as well as the strategic identification.

But later on, I started to realize that what we had was not an interoperability problem. We chopped over $10, $15, $20 mil into campaigns and growth initiatives with very little stickiness. And we have a bunch of executives, and I was the one to do an analysis and basically say, we have lost the blockchain war. We have calcified network effects within the likes of Ethereum, and we can't spend our way into this. The marginal cost of spending capital to bring on new integrations and campaigns is too high. We need to rethink our strategy as a whole.

So what I suggested is pivoting into a financial player alongside my CFO who primarily led and presented this, but I was his right-hand person. That we pivot into a financial player. So I did a deep analysis on the real world asset space as well as some of the other things that we're doing. And we take on a different approach — that we take on counterparty risk. So we set up entities and structure ourselves like a regulated proper fund, with the caveat that we take on this counterparty risk to bring yield to Bitcoin.

And the product that I'm working on is a lending and borrowing one, but we think that it's interesting because when you go to the likes of AAVE or Morpho, or any other lending or borrowing protocol, there is no accountability when there is a hack and institutions don't deal like this, especially the major ones that we want to work with.

So this is what I'm currently designing with our executive team. The fundamental thesis is that those who are long Bitcoin don't want to sell their Bitcoin, but at the same time they need guarantees that their principal will not be lost. That is the key for them. And they need to borrow dollars. They are long Bitcoin. They have short term needs. One clear group that we identified for this is Bitcoin miners. This is literally in their business model. They know that they're going to get Bitcoin in the future. And in the short term, they want to expand their rigs and operations and use their existing Bitcoin as collateral.

On the other side, you have people with stablecoins who just want yield. So what we're proposing is a counterparty model where we work with both parties and take on this risk using the blockchain as infrastructure. And the insurance and counterparty model is the hook, and then we end up using our own infrastructure in the back end. And because this has a counterparty, they don't care about what tokens are used. So we don't have the cold start problem in trying to scale these tokens. We attract liquidity through that way.

At the same time, we are well capitalized because we raise through Bitcoin, so we have a lot of money, but we've been dumping probably $40-50 million per year for the last 5 years and not getting any traction. So this is a pivot away from that model, from pure tech to financial.

What I'm doing is I am more the operations guy who is pulling together the head of financial design. I'm working with scoping out, speaking to customers, generating some leads through our portfolio."
