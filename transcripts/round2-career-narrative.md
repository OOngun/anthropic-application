### On Working with Adeo Resi, Paul Bragiel, and the Career Arc

**Q: What did working 1:1 with Adeo Resi look like day to day? What did you learn? What was the most significant thing you accomplished?**

"It was incredible. I told Jim, who I had a couple of mentoring sessions, and I summarized it in the following way. In Anna Karenina, Tolstoy starts by saying, all happy families are alike. Each unhappy family is unhappy in their own unique way. And I took that in the same way as startups and companies as well, in that the principle is when everything is working properly and when someone is a seasoned veteran and top of their field — I mean, this guy worked with the likes of Elon Musk, and they have many, many stories — everything works the way it should. And I guess that's the Anna Karenina principle in companies as well. So when everything works, it all looks the same.

So I got to experience what greatness looked like and get a real sense of what that greatness looks like — not the overall success, but how it looks day to day and what it takes day to day and what the energy you have to bring and the constant reminding and all of the little things that you need the experience for. It's not something that you can read. It's something that you kind of absorb. It's why people — cooks go staging with some of the best chefs before going out and doing their own thing.

So what are some of the things that I learned? It's that it's nothing grandiose in that you don't have to be this genius who has this great idea and then everything fits into place. It's just showing up every hour, every day, every hour of that day with the intensity and all the hard work that goes into it. It's what a small functional team can achieve when everyone is on board. There's no egos. Everyone is kind of running in the same direction. What all of that looks like. That's one of the things that I learned.

The most significant thing that I accomplished personally was shipping the domicile report. So actually, Paul is the one who introduced me to Adeo because when I worked at SMOK, his fund, I wrote a domicile report for SMOK Ventures, because their partners didn't know what the best domicile was. And they don't want to spend a bunch of money on lawyers. And to be honest, there's no clear guidelines. So I shipped a European domicile report and recommended that they go with the small alternative investment fund manager in The Netherlands, which they ended up going with.

And Adeo was writing the same thing for VC Lab, which is an accelerator to launch venture capital firms. So I think Paul was very impressed, he showed Adeo that piece of work, and they wanted to meet with me. So it was through a direct piece of work that I did that aligned with what they were doing.

So yeah, I then worked to ship, working with legal teams and fund managers within the accelerator, domicile reports that you can still read today on VC Lab. [Reference: https://govclab.com/the-domicile-report/]

This is something that I wrote mostly and led and contributed to mostly. They had the US pretty much nailed down. I shipped the likes of Asia, Netherlands — this one I wrote — Hong Kong, Ontario. I don't think I did Mauritius, Luxembourg, United Kingdom.

As a company, we launched, while I was there, over 250 new VC firms and ran 3 cohorts, which was incredibly rewarding."

**Q: How do you frame your career progression?**

"I decided that I wanted to work in venture capital on the investment side. I then got really hands-on actually launching VC firms at VC Lab. I worked with the likes of Adeo Resi, who is a very well-known, high-profile Silicon Valley founder. I worked with him 1 to 1 for just under a year. He's affiliated with the likes of Elon Musk. I then got a chance to work with Paul Bragiel in one of his funds, SMOK Ventures. He is one of the first investors into Uber, and he sat on the advisory board.
