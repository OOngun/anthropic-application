# EMEA Startup Partnership Analytics Dashboard
## Ongun Ozdemir — March 2026

### What This Is
An interactive analytics dashboard that demonstrates how I would measure and optimise Anthropic's EMEA startup partnership portfolio. Built to show SQL proficiency, Hex dashboard capability, and analytical thinking — three requirements in the Startup Partnerships EMEA job description.

### The Question It Answers
Anthropic deploys API credits to startup partners. The dashboard answers: **is that investment working, and where should the partnerships team focus?**

### Framework
Adapted from Tribe Capital's growth accounting methodology (originally developed at Social Capital) — the same quantitative framework used by top-tier VCs to assess product-market fit. Applied here to evaluate partnership health rather than individual company metrics.

### Three Tiers

**Pulse** — Portfolio health check. Four headline metrics, growth accounting waterfall, CMGR trends, and revenue share distribution. Answers "is the portfolio healthy?" in under ten seconds.

**Partners** — Navigation layer. Sortable scoreboard with colour-coded health signals and a Power Law Tracker ranking partners by projected scale. Answers "who needs my attention?"

**Company Detail** — Full drill-down. Growth accounting decomposition, cohort LTV curves, developer retention analysis, model mix evolution, and credit-to-paid conversion economics. Answers "what's actually happening with this partner and what should I do about it?"

### Case Studies
Three real EMEA AI startups analysed through the framework:
- **Lovable** (Stockholm) — AI app builder, $6.6B valuation. Represents a high-growth, high-retention flagship partner.
- **Dust** (Paris) — AI agent platform. Represents an enterprise-focused, steady-growth mid-tier partner.
- **Cursor** (SF/global) — AI code editor. Represents a developer-tool partner with deep API integration.

### Technical Details
- Synthetic data modelled on real partner characteristics (API usage, revenue, developer activity)
- SQL-driven data generation with growth accounting identities
- Interactive HTML dashboard (Hex-style visualisation)
- Analytical frameworks: Tribe Capital 8-Ball, David Sacks' Burn Multiple, Bessemer efficiency metrics

### Why I Built This
The job description asks for "SQL proficiency and Hex dashboard capability." Rather than just listing SQL as a skill on my CV, I built the dashboard I would actually use in the role — applying institutional-grade VC analytics to the problem of measuring startup partnership ROI.
