## 1. Typical Daily/Weekly Activities

### What the Anthropic JD Explicitly States
The role is described as a **"0-to-1 opportunity"** for **"builders, not traditional partnerships hires."** Day-to-day breaks into five workstreams:

**Workstream A: Startup Sourcing & Engagement (~30% of time)**
- Identifying and prioritising top AI-native startups across EMEA
- Running tiered engagement programs: 1:1 advisory for top-tier partners, 1:many events (hackathons, builder summits, demo days) for the long tail
- Evaluating startup applications to the Claude Startup Program (backed by partner VCs)
- Providing direct technical guidance to founders on Claude integration — "someone founders see as a peer and trusted technical partner"

**Workstream B: VC & Ecosystem Relationship Management (~25% of time)**
- Owning end-to-end relationships with tier-1 VCs (Lightspeed, Accel, a16z, Sequoia, and in EMEA: Partech, Elaia, Eurazeo, Serena Capital, Breega, Singular)
- Negotiating partnership terms and designing exclusive benefits for portfolio companies
- Co-designing programs with accelerators (YC, Station F/F-ai programme, etc.)
- Speaking at ecosystem events and representing Anthropic at startup gatherings

**Workstream C: Analytics & Program Measurement (~20% of time)**
- Building and maintaining Hex dashboards independently (SQL required)
- Tracking usage growth, cohort retention, credits ROI, pipeline conversion
- Writing data-driven reports with insights for internal and external stakeholders
- Shaping strategy through rigorous analysis — "hands-on with data"

**Workstream D: Cross-Functional Coordination (~15% of time)**
- Gathering product feedback from startups to inform Anthropic's roadmap
- Partnering with Sales, Solutions, Product, Events, and Marketing on integrated activation strategies
- Coordinating with cloud partners (AWS, Google Cloud) on joint startup programs
- Creating enablement content and technical trainings

**Workstream E: Program Design & GTM Strategy (~10% of time)**
- Designing compelling startup-focused offerings and pricing models
- Building the startup GTM playbook for new EMEA markets (France, Southern Europe, DACH)
- Creating content marketing and startup-focused campaigns

### Comparative: OpenAI VC Partnerships Manager
OpenAI's equivalent role (VC Partnerships Manager) has similar contours but with key differences:
- More emphasis on managing relationships with VC funds and accelerators
- Explicitly asks for founder/operator experience ("preferably a technical founder")
- Focuses on identifying "1:many engagement" opportunities
- Requires analysing key metrics within startup portfolios and creating reports
- Less emphasis on independent analytics infrastructure (no Hex/SQL requirement mentioned)

### Comparative: Google Cloud Startup Partnerships
- More structured around existing programs (Google for Startups Cloud AI Accelerator — 10 weeks, equity-free)
- Heavier emphasis on credits management ($10K+ credits packages, $150K+ for strategic VC partnerships like Lightspeed)
- Broader scope: workshops, 1:1 guidance from Google experts, Demo Days
- More institutional/less scrappy than Anthropic's "builder" culture

### Comparative: AWS Global Startup Program
- Invite-only program for funded, PMF-stage startups ready to scale
- Assigned Partner Development Managers (PDMs) and Partner Solution Architects (PSAs)
- Three pillars: product development, go-to-market, co-sell
- Team includes former founders, CTOs, VCs, and angel investors
- Up to $1M in credits for accelerator participants (Generative AI Accelerator)

## 2. Key Metrics They're Measured On

### Primary KPIs (derived from JD + industry research)

**Usage Growth:**
- API token consumption growth among startup partners (month-over-month, cohort-based)
- Number of startups actively building on Claude (MAUs of the program)
- Token consumption per startup (expansion signal)
- For context: OpenAI reported API usage hit 1.4B tokens/minute in late 2024, up from 200M at start of year. API reasoning token consumption per org grew 320x YoY.

**Pipeline & Acquisition:**
- Number of new startup partners onboarded per quarter
- VC partnership pipeline: new VCs signed, portfolio companies activated
- Credits-to-conversion rate: % of startups that convert from credits to paid usage
- Time-to-first-API-call after program admission

**Revenue & Expansion:**
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) within startup portfolio — the "give-to-get" ROI
- Expansion revenue: startups that grow from starter credits into $100K+ annual accounts
- Anthropic's own benchmark: EMEA large accounts ($100K+ ARR) grew 10x in the past year
- Gross margin contribution from startup segment

**Retention & Health:**
- Cohort retention curves (empirical LTV)
- Churn rate among credited startups
- Quick Ratio (new + expansion / churn + contraction)
- Burn multiple of partner startups (indicator of partner health/sustainability)

**Ecosystem:**
- Number of VC partnerships active and portfolio companies activated
- Event attendance and post-event conversion metrics
- NPS/satisfaction scores from startup partners
- Product feedback loop: % of startup feedback that influences product roadmap

### How This Maps to Anthropic's Business

Anthropic's revenue hit ~$19B annualized by early 2026. Key context:
- 70-75% of revenue comes from API calls (the startup partnerships channel is directly revenue-generating)
- 500+ customers spending $1M+/year
- 300K+ business customers
- EMEA is the fastest-growing region: **9x revenue growth** and **10x growth in $100K+ accounts** year-over-year
- The startup segment is strategically critical: today's seed-stage Claude user is tomorrow's $1M account

5. **Community access** — office hours, Anthropic team access, community events, direct communication channels
6. **Ongoing engagement** — usage monitoring, growth advisory, expansion opportunities
7. **Graduation** — transition from credits to standard API pricing with guidance on cost optimisation

## 4. Balance Between Technical Advisory and Commercial/BD Work

### The Anthropic Distinction

Anthropic's JD explicitly differentiates this role from traditional BD: **"This role is for builders — someone founders see as a peer and trusted technical partner, not a traditional partnerships hire."**

The split is roughly **60% technical-advisory / 40% commercial-BD**, which is unusual in the industry. Most platform companies (AWS, Google Cloud) skew 30/70 the other way.

**Technical Advisory (60%):**
- Speaking credibly about Claude's capabilities to technical audiences
- Helping founders architect their Claude integration (prompt engineering, model selection, agent design)
- Understanding AI/ML technology landscape deeply enough to advise on build decisions
- Creating technical enablement content and trainings
- Running hands-on workshops and hackathons
- Gathering detailed product feedback (requires technical understanding to interpret)
- Building own analytics (SQL + Hex) rather than relying on a data team

**Commercial/BD (40%):**
- Negotiating VC partnership terms
- Designing pricing models and credit structures for startups
- Pipeline management and conversion tracking
- Event planning and ecosystem marketing
- GTM strategy and market expansion (new EMEA geographies)
- Coordinating with Sales on account prioritisation and handoffs

### Why Anthropic Skews Technical

1. **The product is an API** — startup partners are developers; credibility requires technical fluency
2. **The "builder" culture** — Anthropic's internal culture is engineering-first; non-technical partners would struggle to earn trust
3. **Self-service analytics** — no dedicated data analyst for the startup team; you build your own Hex dashboards
4. **Feedback quality** — turning vague product complaints into actionable engineering feedback requires deep understanding
5. **The competitive landscape** — OpenAI, Google, and AWS all have technical SA/solutions teams; Anthropic bundles this into the partnerships role

### Comparative: OpenAI
- More split: has separate "VC Partnerships Manager" (commercial-heavy) and "Technical Program Manager, Strategic Partnership" (technical-heavy)
- VC Partnerships Manager is still expected to provide "technical guidance to startups in 1:1 engagements"

### Comparative: AWS
- Clearly bifurcated: Partner Development Managers (PDMs, commercial) and Partner Solution Architects (PSAs, technical)
- Startup partnerships people coordinate both but don't need to be both

### Comparative: Google Cloud
- Structured around existing program rails (accelerators, credits programs)
- Google experts provide 1:1 technical guidance separately from BD/partnerships
- More institutional separation between technical and commercial functions

## 6. What "Success" Looks Like in the First 6-12 Months

### First 90 Days: Learn, Map, Quick Wins

- **Learn the product deeply:** Get hands-on with Claude API, understand capabilities vs. competitors, know the pricing model cold
- **Map the EMEA ecosystem:** Identify top 50-100 AI-native startups, map VC relationships, understand which partners are already on Claude
- **Build your analytics foundation:** Set up Hex dashboards for usage tracking, pipeline management, and program measurement
- **Quick wins:** Onboard 5-10 high-signal startups, run first builder event, close first VC partnership
- **Build internal relationships:** Establish working rhythms with Sales, Product, Solutions, Marketing

### Months 3-6: Build the Machine

- **Launch tiered engagement program:** Define clear tiers (credits, rate limits, advisory access) mapped to startup stage/potential
- **Establish 3-5 VC partnerships:** Formalize partnerships with EMEA VCs, launch portfolio activation programs
- **Run signature events:** First London Builder Summit, first EMEA hackathon, co-hosted events with VCs
- **Create feedback flywheel:** Systematise how startup feedback reaches Product team
- **Hit pipeline targets:** 20-50 startups actively building on Claude, clear conversion funnel from credits to paid

### Months 6-12: Scale and Prove ROI

- **Demonstrate give-to-get ROI:** Show that credits invested are generating sustainable revenue
- **Grow usage metrics:** Measurable increase in EMEA startup API consumption
- **Produce case studies:** 3-5 compelling stories of startups building transformative products with Claude
- **Expand geographically:** Support new EMEA market launches (France, DACH, Southern Europe — coordinating with local hires)
- **Influence product:** At least 2-3 product improvements that trace back to startup feedback you championed
- **Build the playbook:** Document repeatable processes that the expanding team can follow

## Key Takeaways for Your Application

1. **The role is unusually technical for partnerships.** Anthropic wants someone who can write SQL, build Hex dashboards, advise founders on Claude architecture, AND manage VC relationships. Your VC background + growing technical skills (SQL, Python, Claude usage) is the right combination — lean into both.

2. **"0-to-1" is the defining characteristic.** Your experience building the $5.5M to $15.5M grant program from scratch at RootstockLabs is directly analogous. Frame it as: "I've done 0-to-1 program building before, with real money, at a deep-tech company."

3. **The VC network matters enormously.** Your Ada Ventures scout role, VC Lab, SMOK Ventures, and Portfolio Ventures experience gives you an authentic entry point into the VC relationship management part of the role. You understand how VCs think about portfolio support.

4. **EMEA is the growth story.** 9x revenue growth makes this the most exciting region at Anthropic right now. They're building the team from scratch across UK, France, DACH, Southern Europe. Joining now means defining the playbook.

5. **Guillaume Princen's background is your blueprint.** He was Stripe's first European employee, built operations from 0-to-1 across 12 offices. He will value someone who understands what early-stage market building looks like. Your RootstockLabs experience (small team, big impact, strategic pivots) speaks this language.

6. **The Hex dashboard project you're building is exactly right.** The JD literally says "SQL proficient and comfortable building their own Hex dashboards." Sending a working dashboard with synthetic data modelled on real partners (Lovable, Dust, Cursor) proves the hardest-to-fake requirement.
